[E-Commerce] Customer Churn Prediction Analysis
Ho Thanh Xuan · Final Project · Machine Learning

Purpose of this document:

This document presents a comprehensive analysis of customer churn behavior in an e-commerce platform. The objective is to identify the key factors driving customer churn, build a predictive model using RandomForest, and segment churned users into actionable groups via KMeans clustering to enable targeted retention strategies.

A. Abstract

Customer churn — the loss of existing users — is one of the most critical challenges for any e-commerce business. Acquiring new customers is significantly more expensive than retaining existing ones, making churn prediction and prevention a high-priority task.

Using a dataset of 5,630 customers with 20 behavioral and transactional features, we performed end-to-end analysis including data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), supervised learning (classification), and unsupervised learning (clustering). The final RandomForest model achieved a balanced accuracy of 95.5% after hyperparameter tuning, and KMeans segmentation revealed 4 distinct churn profiles with a silhouette score of 0.425.

Key Finding: Churn is not driven by a single factor but by a combination of tenure, complain, cashbackamount, and product/device preferences. Each churned segment requires a different retention approach.
B. Investigation
1. Data Preparation

The raw dataset contained 5,630 rows × 20 columns loaded from churn_prediction.xlsx. Pre-processing steps included:

  • Missing values (~32% of rows): Since >30% of rows had at least one null, dropping was not viable. Columns tenure, warehousetohome, orderamounthikefromlastyear, ordercount, daysincelastorder were filled with median; hourspendonapp and couponused were filled with 0.
  • Duplicate check: No duplicated rows found.
  • Value harmonization: Merged synonyms — e.g., Mobile Phone → Phone, CC → Credit Card, COD → Cash on Delivery.
  • Class imbalance check: Churn=1 represents the minority class; stratified splitting was applied.

3.4 Unsupervised Learning — KMeans Clustering on Churned Users

To enable targeted retention, churned customers (churn=1) were segmented using KMeans after PCA dimensionality reduction to 3 components. The Elbow Method indicated K=4 as the optimal number of clusters, with a silhouette score of 0.425 (moderate but meaningful structure).

Fig 2. Cluster feature profile — Mean values of key features per segment

Cluster 0 — "Loyal but Silently Disengaging"

High tenure (8.5), high coupon use (4.2), low complain (0.42), 62% married.

Issue: Not price-driven — lacking engagement or competitor pull.

Action: Loyalty program, personalized recommendations.

Cluster 1 — "New, Deal-Sensitive Buyers"

Very low tenure (2.5), low coupon use (1.6), 89% prefer Phone category, 57% single.

Issue: One-time buyers, insufficient value perception.

Action: First-purchase promotions, bundle deals, retargeting ads.

Cluster 2 — "Low-Engagement Mobile Users"

Low app hours (1.85), very low coupon use (0.67), 82% mobile preference.

Issue: Poor mobile UX, no motivation to return.

Action: Push notifications, app UX improvement, gamification.

Cluster 3 — "High-Value but Demanding"

High delivery distance (20), city tier 2.8, 61% laptop, high complain (0.56).

Issue: Slow delivery, unmet high expectations.

Action: Faster delivery, premium support, VIP services.

C. Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates that customer churn in e-commerce is a multi-factorial problem. The key findings are:

  1. Predictive Power: The tuned RandomForest model achieves 95.5% balanced accuracy, making it highly reliable for identifying at-risk customers before they churn.
  2. Top Churn Drivers: tenure (new users churn most), complain (bad experience → immediate churn), and cashbackamount (low incentive → higher churn risk).
  3. Actionable Segments: The 4 churned-user clusters each require distinct retention strategies:
    • Loyal users → enhanced experiences, not discounts
    • New buyers → onboarding promotions and retargeting
    • Mobile users → UX improvements and gamification
    • High-value users → premium delivery and VIP treatment
Final Recommendation: Deploy the RandomForest model to score all active customers weekly, flag high-risk users, and route them to the appropriate retention campaign based on their predicted cluster profile. This data-driven approach enables the company to develop personalized promotion strategies for each segment, improving retention of at-risk customers.
